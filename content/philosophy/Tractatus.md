[[Wittgenstein]]

1. Metaphysics of objects: a world of facts, not things.
	1. The world is a collection of facts (true propositions) not things (objects.)
	2. The world is made up of distinct units of existence. 
		1. This view is arrived at through logic, rather than empirical research.
	3. Thought, language and the world must be structured in the same way. 
2. Picture theory of language.
	1. The function of language is to form pictures of reality.
	2. A picture is a model of reality, not the reality itself.
		1. Thus a picture presents a situation in logical space and is not necessarily corresponding to the way things actually are.
	3. A picture must have its pictorial form in common with reality to be able to depict it.
	4. It is impossible to tell from te picture alone wether it is true of false, we have to compare it to reality.
	5. Language gets its meaning by representing possible states of affairs in logical space.
3. Meaning and meaninglessness.
	1. Tautology and contradiction are not pictures of reality. They present no possible state of affairs.
	2. Philosophy must set limits to what can be thought; and, in doing so, to what cannot be thought.
	3. The limits of my language mean the limits of my world.
	4. There are important things we can't meaningfully say, but we can show them.
4. Purpose of philosophy.

# Tractatus Logico-Philosophicus

## The world is everything that is the case.
> 1.1 The world is the totality of facts, not of things.>
> 1.12 For the totality of facts determines both what is the case, and also all that is not the case.

Met dingen bedoelt Wittgenstein de verzameling van alle objecten. Met feiten bedoelt Wittgenstein de samenhang tussen al die dingen.
De wereld is niet de verzameling van alle dingen {appel, boom, auto} maar de verzameling van alle feiten {"er valt een appel op Newton's hoofd", "De aarde is rond", etc.}

Feiten worden uitgedrukt in zinnen. 

> 1.13 The facts in logical space are the world.

De logische ruimte is de ruimte van alle mogelijkheden. Dus niet alleen wat echt het geval is maar ook wat niet het geval is maar wel logisch mogelijk is. 
De feiten zijn dan de punten in deze ruimte die overeenkomen met de waarheid, of de wereld.

> 1.2 The world divides into facts.
> 1.21 Any one can either be the case or not be the case, and everything else remain the same.

Met feiten bedoelt Wittgenstein hier *atomaire feiten*, dingen die onafhankelijk van elkaar het geval kunnen zijn. Wat zijn die feiten die onafhankelijk van andere feiten zijn? 

## What is the case, the fact, is the existence of atomic facts.

> 2.01 An atomic fact is a combination of objects (entities, things).
> 2.011 It is essential to a thing that it can be a constituent part of an atomic fact.
