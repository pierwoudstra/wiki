The Pythagorean theorem states that the sides of any right triangle can be described by:
$A^2+B^2=C^2$

In a unit circle (a circle with circumference 1), the $(x, y)$ coordinates of angle $\theta$ are:
$(\cos\theta,\sin\theta)$.

Combining this with the Pythagorean theorem we can find that:
$\cos^2\theta + \sin^2\theta = 1$

In the case of an angle of $\pi\over4$ we know that the $x$ and $y$ sides are equal, and that the hypotenuse (or just the circumference) is 1, therefore:
$a^2 + a^2 = 1$
$2(a^2)=1$
$a={\sqrt{1\over2}}={1\over\sqrt2}$

so ${\sin{\pi\over4}}={\cos{\pi\over4}}={1\over\sqrt2}$
